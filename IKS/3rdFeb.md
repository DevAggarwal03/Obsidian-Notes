**Caturdasa - vidyasthana**
- the fourteen branches of learning in Hinduism,
- includes 4 veds, six accessory texts (vedangs),
- Dharmashastra, puranas, mimamsa, and nyaya.
-  4 Vedas - Rig, Yajur, Sama(song, melodies), Atharva(medicine) generally referred to as snatana Dharma
-  Vdangas - six auxiliary disciplines of Vedic studies. Accessory texts or studies of Vedas
	-  Siksha (phonetics)
	-  Kalpa (ordinances),
	-  vyakarana (grammar),
	-  nirkuta (etymology),
	-  Chandas (metrical schience)
	-  jyotisha (astology and astrinomy)

**Vedangas**
- **Shiksha**: Rules for recitiation of Vedic mantras with their proper accent
- **Kalpa**: Rules of households rites, teh rites acording to vedic text, and those governing codes of conduct in family and soc life
- **Vyakarana** (Grammar): strucutre of Vedic lang
- **Nirkuta**: meaning of vedic words
- **Chanda**: seven meters of the vedic language. It refers to sci and poetic meters in Vedic literature, Particularly in sanskrit. Chandas derived from word chad meaning to cover or please.
- **Jyotisha**: know the appropriate time for the performance of different rites.

**Puranas**: 18 in number, explains Sanatana Dharma embedded in the vedas.
**Itihasas**: Historical events occ in specific time line. eg: Mahabarta and Ramayna
**Dharmashastras**: Hindu scriptures that include moral precepts and principles for religious duty and good behaviour. Guide to lead dharmic life with principles based on vedas.
**Smriti**: Rule book of guidelines of how to put dharmic principles in action in life and what will be conseq of not doing so.
